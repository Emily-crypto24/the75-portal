Meeting One · Online Google Meet · 30 Minutes

First Planning
Meeting

This is where your vision takes shape. We will uncover your wedding style, priorities, and the details that make your day uniquely yours — all in a relaxed, guided conversation.

What to Expect

The Vision Meeting

Your first planning meeting is a 30-minute online Google Meet designed to be a conversation, not an interrogation. Your lead coordinator will guide you through your ideas, answer early questions, and begin building the foundation of your wedding day plan.

Before this meeting, you will complete your Planning Form — a beautiful digital questionnaire that captures all the key details in advance so your coordinator arrives prepared. This means your meeting time is spent discussing, refining, and getting excited together rather than filling out paperwork.

What We Will Cover

  • Couple & Event Basics — Names, wedding date, ceremony and reception times, guest count, family dynamics
  • Vision & Priorities — Your style, theme, color palette, what matters most to you and your guests
  • Ceremony Dreams — Location preference, backdrop, traditions, who is walking, ceremony vibe and seating
  • Vendor Introductions — Who you are considering for catering, photography, florals, DJ, transportation, and more
  • The Box Hops — Priority access for you and your guests to our on-property and nearby Airbnbs
  • Using The 75 Décor — Overview of décor available from our inventory
  • Overview of Next Steps — What to tackle before your Second Planning Meeting

Before This Meeting: Your Homework

  • Complete your First Planning Meeting Form (link in your email or below)
  • Begin researching vendors from The Seventy Five Preferred Vendor List
  • Start gathering inspiration — Pinterest boards, style ideas, color palettes
  • Decide on caterer early — this is the most important and fills up fastest
  • Think about which Box Hops you may want to reserve for your guests
  • Review the Wedding Guide sent with your booking confirmation

After This Meeting: Your Next Steps

  • Confirm priority vendors including arrival and pickup times
  • Pick out décor items you would like to use or rent from The 75
  • Choose linen colors to see at your in-person Second Planning Meeting
  • Choose your floor plan for ceremony and reception tables
  • Decide which Box Hops, if any, you would like to book
Action Required Before Meeting

Complete Your Planning Form

Fill out your First Planning Meeting Form before your scheduled meeting. Your answers auto-save as you go — do not hit Submit until your coordinator reviews it with you.

Meeting Two · In Person at The Seventy Five · Halfway Point

Second Planning
Meeting

This is your "locking it in" meeting. By the end of today, your wedding will start feeling less like a dream and more like a plan you cannot wait to celebrate.

Bringing Your Vision to Life

The Design & Details Meeting

Your Second Planning Meeting is held in person at The Seventy Five — and it is the most exciting one. This is where everything stops being ideas and starts becoming real. Bring décor pieces you are considering, and we will set the table so you can see your vision come to life in the actual space.

Come ready to make decisions. By the end of this meeting, we will have locked in the majority of your details — which means your Final Planning Meeting becomes a confident confirmation, not a scramble.

📋 Bring Your Security Deposit

Please bring a physical check of $1,000 for your security deposit to this meeting, made out to The Seventy Five Venue, Attn: Venue Manager, 10835 State Route 374, Rockbridge, Ohio 43149. This check is held and shredded the day after your event if no damages occur.

What We Will Cover

  • Design & Décor — Table place settings, décor items from The 75, linens per table, candles, add-on décor staff tiers
  • Linen Selections — Napkin color, fold style, guest table linens, head/bridal party table linens, cocktail table linens
  • Vendor Check-In — Confirm who is booked, arrival times, pickup times, catering plan, cake details
  • Your People & Flow — Finance person, MOH, Best Man, who takes food/cake/alcohol/décor home, accessibility needs
  • Order of Events — Grand entrance format, events of the evening, rain plan, shuttle logistics
  • Box Hops Confirmation — Finalize which properties you are booking
  • Catering Details — Cocktail hour appetizers, dinner style, late night snacks, post-ceremony treats for couple

Before This Meeting: Your Homework

  • Confirm all priority vendors and have arrival/pickup times ready
  • Pick out décor items from The 75 inventory you want to use
  • Browse linens at linenshowroom.com and come with color ideas
  • Decide on your floor plan for ceremony and reception
  • Complete your Second Planning Meeting Form before arriving
  • Book your Box Hops if you have not already — send the link to guests
  • Bring any décor items you want to physically see in the space

After This Meeting: Your Next Steps

  • Book any remaining Box Hops or hotel blocks for guests
  • Finalize and confirm any outstanding vendors
  • Draft and finalize ceremony details to share with your officiant
  • Finalize all table décor and notify The 75 of any changes
  • Share inspiration photos so The 75 team can curate décor to your vision
  • Confirm guest transportation if needed

Meeting Three · Virtual Google Meet · 4–6 Weeks Before Your Wedding

Final Planning
Meeting

The alignment call that brings everyone to the table. Your lead coordinator, caterer, DJ, and photographer join together to confirm every last detail — so your entire team walks into your wedding day fully in sync.

The Final Alignment

Take a Breath — You Are Ready.

Your Final Planning Meeting is a virtual Google Meet held 4 to 6 weeks before your wedding. But this one is different — it is not just between you and your coordinator. Your caterer, DJ, and photographer are all invited to attend so every member of your core vendor team can hear the plan, ask questions, and get aligned together.

You have done the heavy lifting. Your vendors are secured, your design is set, and your timeline is nearly complete. This meeting is about pulling everything together into one clear plan so you walk into your wedding week with complete confidence.

Who Attends This Meeting

Your lead coordinator will send Google Meet invites to you, your partner, and your core vendors. Make sure the following contacts have confirmed attendance before your meeting date.

You + Your Partner Lead Coordinator Caterer DJ / Band Photographer

What We Will Cover

  • Setup & All Arrival Times — Every vendor, the bridal party, guests, shuttles — timed to the minute
  • Vendor Confirmation — Final confirmation of all booked vendors, what each is bringing, pickup times
  • Ceremony Lineup — Full walking order, who escorts whom, grandparents, flower girls, ring bearers, family seating
  • Special Logistics — Pre-ceremony guest access, reception curtain, bar policy, couple's private moment, champagne service
  • Day-of Timeline — Every moment from venue open to final send-off, confirmed with all vendors on the call
  • Rain Plan Confirmed — Final indoor backup plan locked and communicated to all vendors
  • End-of-Night Logistics — Who takes food, cake, alcohol, and décor home; special send-off details
  • Prismm Floor Plan — Final approval of your table layout before linens are ordered

Before This Meeting: Your Final Checklist

  • Confirm all vendor payments are scheduled or paid
  • Review all planning forms to ensure they are fully filled out and accurate
  • Confirm rehearsal arrival time with all parties (officiant, bridal party, parents)
  • Share the ceremony walking order with your bridal party
  • Share the day-of timeline with your bridal party and key point people
  • Pack your day-of bags with all personal items
  • Plan a date night before the big day — you deserve it!
